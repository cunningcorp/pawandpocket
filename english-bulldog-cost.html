How much does an English Bulldog really cost?

All the charm, and one of the heaviest health bills in dogdom. Here's the honest 2026 breakdown — including the part most guides skip.

English Bulldogs (British Bulldogs) are gentle, comical, hugely loveable — and, being honest, one of the most expensive breeds to keep healthy. Like the French Bulldog they're flat-faced, but the English Bulldog takes the health challenges further, and it shows up in both the vet bills and a notably short lifespan.

The short answer: an English Bulldog puppy usually runs $1,500–$4,500 (rare colours far more), plus roughly $2,000–$4,000 a year to keep — putting the lifetime cost around $22,000–$42,000 across a relatively short 8–10 year life. High costs packed into fewer years is the story here.

The upfront cost

From a reputable breeder, expect $1,500–$4,500, with tri-colour and rare-colour pups running to $8,500 or beyond. As with the Frenchie, the price reflects difficult breeding: English Bulldogs almost always need artificial insemination and C-section births (their heads are large and hips narrow), so breeders carry heavy veterinary costs. Rescue is cheaper and rehomes do come up. Day-one setup runs a little higher than most at $500–$1,000, since this breed needs things like cooling beds and wide bowls to suit their build.

Why an English Bulldog costs so much to keep

This is the section that matters most, because the English Bulldog is medically complex even by flat-faced standards — one study found the breed more likely to have at least one health disorder than dogs in general. The big cost drivers:

  • Breathing problems (BOAS) — nearly every English Bulldog has some degree of brachycephalic airway trouble, and corrective airway surgery typically runs $1,800–$3,500. It's the signature Bulldog cost.
  • Hip dysplasia — the breed has the highest recorded rate of hip dysplasia of any dog, so joint issues are common and can mean major surgery.
  • Skin-fold dermatitis — those famous wrinkles trap moisture and infection, needing routine cleaning and recurring vet visits to keep on top of.
  • Eye conditions — cherry eye and dry eye (KCS) are both common and often need medication or a minor procedure.
  • Heat intolerance — their airways make them dangerously poor at cooling down, so a hot day is a genuine emergency risk, not just discomfort.

The yearly running costs

  • Food — a stocky medium dog runs roughly $400–$700 a year, and keeping them lean genuinely matters, as extra weight worsens every other issue.
  • Routine vet care — higher than average at about $500–$1,000+ a year, since Bulldogs need extra airway and skin checks even when healthy.
  • Grooming — low on clipping but budget $150–$300 for the essential skin-fold and wrinkle care this breed can't go without.
  • Insurance — among the priciest of any breed, often $70–$100+ a month, because insurers know how medically demanding Bulldogs are.

A typical year lands around $2,000–$4,000, and with this breed a big airway or orthopaedic bill is more a question of when than if.

Is insurance worth it for an English Bulldog?

For an English Bulldog, insurance is one of the most justifiable buys in all of dog ownership — this is a breed where significant vet bills are close to expected, not a rare misfortune. The catch is that many of the breed's problems appear early, so enrol the moment you get your puppy, before airway, skin, or eye issues become pre-existing exclusions, and check the policy doesn't exclude brachycephalic conditions, which are the very things you need covered.
